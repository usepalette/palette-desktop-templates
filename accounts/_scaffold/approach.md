# Sales Approach

How you sell — the philosophy, discovery questions, and qualification signals
the `/account prep` mode draws on so briefs sound like *you*, not a generic
playbook. Personalized at install; edit it as your motion evolves.

## Philosophy

Understand the customer's world before talking about yours. Discovery first,
demo second. If you can't articulate their problem better than they can, you
haven't earned the right to pitch.

**Core beliefs:**
- The best sales conversations feel like consulting, not pitching.
- Customers buy when they feel understood, not when they feel impressed.
- Specifics beat superlatives — "{{PROOF_POINT}}" beats "revolutionary".
- If {{COMPANY}} isn't right for them, say so early. Trust compounds.

## Before the call

- Read everything in the account folder (`accounts/<slug>/`).
- Check their website, LinkedIn, recent news.
- Have a hypothesis about their problem — but hold it loosely.
- Prepare discovery questions, not a slide deck.

## During the call

- **Discovery (60-70%):** start with their world. Open-ended questions about
  how they work today. Listen for pain language — write it in their words.
- **Positioning (20-30%):** connect their specific pain to what you do. Use
  their language. Show the workflow that addresses the pain. Be honest about
  what's live vs. coming.
- **Next steps (5-10%):** always end with a specific next action, not "let's
  stay in touch".

## After the call

- Log it with `/account meeting <slug>`.
- Lift follow-ups into the account's Open threads.
- Send a follow-up within 24 hours.

## Qualification

**Good signals:**
- They describe a real pain, not theoretical interest.
- They've tried to solve it before (urgency).
- The person on the call can influence the decision.
- They ask detailed questions about how it works.

**Red flags:**
- "Just exploring" with no specific pain.
- No budget authority and no path to it.
- They want a feature you don't have and won't build.
- The timeline is "someday".

## What we don't do

- Cold pitch without research.
- Lead with a demo before understanding the problem.
- Promise timelines we can't keep.
- Discount to close — if price is the only objection, it's not the right fit.
- Use pressure tactics ("limited time offer", "others are signing up").
